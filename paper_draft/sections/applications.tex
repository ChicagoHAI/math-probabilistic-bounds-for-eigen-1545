\section{Applications}\label{sec:applications}

Our results have several important applications:

\subsection{Covariance Estimation}

For high-dimensional data $X_1,\ldots,X_n \in \R^p$, our bounds imply that the sample covariance matrix concentrates around the population covariance in spectral norm:

\begin{corollary}
Let $\hat{\Sigma}_n$ be the sample covariance matrix and $\Sigma$ the population covariance. Then:
\[ \Prob(\spectral{\hat{\Sigma}_n - \Sigma} \geq t) \leq 2\exp(-cnt^2) \]
\end{corollary}

\subsection{Random Graphs}

For the adjacency matrix $A$ of an Erdős-Rényi random graph, our results give tight bounds on the second eigenvalue:

\begin{corollary}
Let $G(n,p)$ be an Erdős-Rényi random graph with $p \geq c\log n/n$. Then:
\[ \Prob(|\lambda_2(A)| \geq 2\sqrt{np(1-p)} + t) \leq 2\exp(-ct^2) \]
\end{corollary}

These bounds have implications for spectral clustering and community detection algorithms.
